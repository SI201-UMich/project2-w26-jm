I believe that the current system for regulating short-term rentals in San Francisco is not entirely adequate in ensuring full accountability. 
This is because even though hosts are required to register 
with the city’s planning office, and Airbnb is expected to display registration numbers, 
this process relies strongly on self-reporting and voluntary platform enforcement. This lack of rigor in the
 process can allow for non-compliant listings to operate undetected. An improvement that could make this more error
proof would be a mandatory 
verification by the platform before listing a property. This would make sure fewer people are able to go undetected
 through the process and create responsibility on all parties involved to prevent harm.
One important factor when considering the legality of web scraping is whether the 
scraped data violates the terms of service or access restrictions of a website. Even if 
the data is publicly available, it may not be legal to access and scrape it depending 
on the company's site policy and governmental structures. This matters alot becuase 
it shows the tension between public access to information and the legal rights of the 
website owners, so scraping has to adhere to both legal policy and platform rules.

Avoid scraping personal data without explicit permission, as this could violate people's privacy.
Look at any website information regarding whether they state their policies on taking data from their website.
